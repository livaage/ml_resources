Reference Textbooks

The canonical books for when the TL;DRs aren't enough. Many are free PDFs online.

General ML & deep learning

  • Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville — Deep Learning Free Book The classic deep learning text. Free HTML version. Slightly dated on modern architectures (no transformers in depth) but the foundations chapters (6–8) are still the standard reference.
  • Hastie, Tibshirani, Friedman — Elements of Statistical Learning Free Book The classical-ML reference. Free PDF. Dense but exhaustive — every section eventually gets cited somewhere.
  • James, Witten, Hastie, Tibshirani — Introduction to Statistical Learning Free Book Friendlier little sibling of ESL. Free PDF, with Python and R versions. Best entry point for classical ML with statistical rigor.
  • Murphy — Probabilistic Machine Learning Free Book Modern, comprehensive, free. Two volumes (foundations + advanced). Strongest treatment of probabilistic methods. Vol 1 is the default modern ML textbook.
  • Bishop — Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning Free Book 2006 but still relevant. Beautiful Bayesian-flavoured treatment. Free PDF. The clearest source on EM, mixture models, and variational methods.

Specialty topics

  • Sutton & Barto — Reinforcement Learning Free Book The standard RL textbook. Free PDF. Foundational for understanding policy gradients, value functions, and the modern derivatives.
  • Jurafsky & Martin — Speech and Language Processing Free Book 3rd edition, in progress, free draft. The canonical NLP textbook. Covers everything from regex to transformers.
  • Rasmussen & Williams — Gaussian Processes for ML Free Book Free PDF. The reference on GPs from regression to classification to sparse approximations.
  • Molnar — Interpretable Machine Learning Free Book The reference for SHAP, LIME, partial dependence, counterfactual explanations. Free online book.
  • Barocas, Hardt, Narayanan — Fairness and ML Free Book The standard text on fairness, accountability, and ethical considerations in ML.

Practitioner-oriented

  • Huyen — Designing Machine Learning Systems Paid Book Chip Huyen on the engineering of ML systems — data, deployment, monitoring, drift. The best single book on putting ML in production.
  • Lakshmanan, Robinson, Munn — Machine Learning Design Patterns Paid Book Patterns and anti-patterns from real production systems. Pairs well with Huyen.
